# Product Context

## Register

Brand surface. Design is the impression: a personal portfolio, blog, and forkable Nuxt template for a full-stack engineer and open-source contributor.

## Purpose

Pahachaan presents Akshara Hegde's engineering work, writing, tools, and public projects in a compact, content-first format. It should help visitors quickly understand what Akshara builds, where his strengths are, and which project, article, or contact path is worth opening next.

The site also acts as a reusable portfolio template for developers who want a content-driven personal site powered by Nuxt, Nuxt Content, Nuxt Studio, and Nuxt UI.

## Audience

- Technical founders, engineering teams, recruiters, and collaborators evaluating Akshara's work.
- Open-source peers and developers interested in AI-powered web apps, developer tools, Nuxt, Python, and LLM workflows.
- Developers looking for a portfolio template they can fork, customize, and maintain through content files.

## Positioning

Minimal technical portfolio for a builder who ships practical software, writes about implementation details, and contributes to open source. The strongest signal is not self-promotion; it is the work itself.

## Voice

Minimal, technical, calm.

Use direct language, concrete nouns, and short sentences. Prefer proof through projects, artifacts, roles, and links. Avoid hype, inflated claims, or generic productivity framing.

## Design Principles

- Content before ornament: projects, writing, contact links, and status cues should remain immediately scannable.
- Developer-native feel: visual cues may borrow from terminals, docs, GitHub profiles, and technical writing.
- Compact density: keep the layout efficient without feeling cramped.
- Template-aware: preserve patterns that make the site easy for other developers to customize through `content/`.
- Personal but not theatrical: the interface should feel authored, calm, and precise.

## References

- Developer documentation and terminal tools.
- GitHub profiles and open-source project pages.
- Technical blogs with strong reading rhythm.
- Type specimen pages and editorial portfolios where typography carries the experience.

## Anti-References

- Generic SaaS gradients, glass cards, oversized hero claims, and vague productivity language.
- Corporate resume-site styling or stiff career-copy tone.
- Over-animated creative portfolio effects that obscure reading or navigation.
- Decorative UI that competes with project titles, descriptions, or article metadata.

## Accessibility Priorities

- Strong readability in both light and dark mode.
- Keyboard-friendly navigation and visible interactive states.
- Meaningful contrast for body copy, secondary metadata, badges, and links.
- Reduced visual noise and restraint with motion.

## Keep

- The small-site information architecture: home, projects, blog, uses, resources, and shop.
- Content-driven customization through JSON and Markdown.
- Light/dark mode and theme customization as user-facing strengths.
- Fast loading, minimal layout, and clear project/article lists.

## Avoid

- Turning the homepage into a large marketing landing page.
- Adding unnecessary animations, decorative panels, or visual effects.
- Diluting Akshara's work with broad claims that are not backed by content.
- Making template customization harder by burying content in component code.
